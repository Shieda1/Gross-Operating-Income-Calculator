What is the gross operating income?
the gross operating income is a measurement of a company's profitability that includes the cost of goods sold (COGS) and excludes interest and income tax expenses.
How to calculate gross operating income?
To calculate gross operating income you should use the following formula:
operating income = total revenue - direct costs - indirect costs
example: company x has a total revenue of $265,000, $38,000 worth of direct costs and the indirect costs are $50,000, calculate the gross operating income.
using the gross operating income the answer will be:
operating income = total revenue - direct costs - indirect costs
operating income = $265,000 - $38,000 - $50,000
operating income = $177,000
Why use gross operating income?
the gross operating income (GOI) is usually used to measure the performance of a fimr's core operations and also to compare it to competitors
